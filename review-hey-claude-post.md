### Step 2: Jamie Gut-Check

Review the draft against these four questions. For each one, provide a brief assessment (1-2 sentences):

**1. Would Jamie feel SEEN by the opening?**
- Does it name a struggle Jamie has experienced?
- Is it personal and specific, not generic?
- Would Jamie think "oh, this is about me" in the first few lines?

**2. Would Jamie understand this without Googling anything?**
- Is there jargon that needs explaining?
- Are technical concepts shown through story rather than defined?
- Would someone who's never used Claude Code still follow along?

**3. Is there something Jamie can try in under 5 minutes?**
- Is there a practical takeaway?
- Is it copy-paste ready or clearly actionable?
- Does it feel accessible, not overwhelming?

**4. Does Jamie leave thinking "I can do it"?**
- Does the post end with possibility, not homework?
- Is the transformation clear â€” from "I can't" to "I can"?
- Does it feel like permission, not instruction?

### Step 3: "What Hey Claude is NOT" Check

Flag if any of these are creeping in:

| Check | Warning Signs |
|-------|---------------|
| **Tutorial creep** | Step-by-step instructions dominate; story is just a wrapper |
| **Neurotypical productivity** | Assumes discipline/willpower; doesn't acknowledge ADHD struggles |
| **AI hype** | "AI will change everything!" without grounding in real experience |
| **AI fear** | Defensive about AI use; over-explaining why it's okay |
| **Listicle in disguise** | "5 ways to..." or "Here's what I learned..." as the structure |
| **Science explainer** | Leading with research/neuroscience instead of personal story |

For each that applies, note specifically where it happens and suggest how to refocus.
